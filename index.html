Before You Start

~15 Mins Total Mobile Preferred

This app replaces field clipboards for shift supervisors to log hazard assessments, signatures, and weather risks in under 5 minutes.

  • Prerequisite: A personal @gmail.com address is required to bypass employee SSO imitation screen.
  • Chained Tasks: Scenarios 1–5 simulate a real workday. Run them in exact sequential order.
  • Data Convention: Use your [Initial] & Sons Landscaping and emails like testworker1@fakeco.com.
Tester Persona Mindset If you aren't a Safety Manager, act like a busy workplace foreman trying to keep workers safe from extreme heat while dodging heavy OSHA regulatory fines.
Onboarding

Scenario 1: Workspace Setup

Role: Owner / Admin Pass Time: < 3 Mins
Story: You just started a small landscaping company in Florida. Before your crew rolls out Monday morning, initialize your app footprint.
  1. Open the target application environment:
    Launch Safety App
  2. Tap "Sign In with Employee Google Identity" and authenticate with your personal Gmail credentials.
  3. Select "Establish a New Workspace (Path A)". Ignore Path B.
  4. Fill out company name using convention: [Your Initial] & Sons Landscaping. Choose any location and team size.
  5. Select Landscaping as the operational category and tap "Next Step".
  6. Leave configuration defaults as-is and hit "Complete & Initialize Workspace".
What to watch for: Did the location engine pause for 3-5 seconds to initialize data? Was Path A's layout clear?
Checklists

Scenario 2: Mid-Day Heat Sweep

Role: Shift Manager Location: Main Dashboard
Story: It's noon, the sun is blazing, and the air is uncomfortably hot. Run a rapid safety verification sweep on-site.
  1. On the main Dashboard, look directly under the Heat Calculator for the card labeled "Log Current Ambient Conditions & Field Context".
  2. Open the dropdown menus and select your current shift conditions:
    • CREW EXERTION LEVEL: Select Moderate Exertion (Walking/Handling)
    • CLOTHING & GEAR PROTECTION: Select Standard Work Uniform
    • SUN EXPOSURE LEVEL: Select Full Shade / Covered Structure
  3. Click open the assessment & log tools, then click Collapse to minimize the widget. Click Expand to bring it back.
  4. Now, tap Checklists on the main navigation bar.
  5. On the default Heat Check form, toggle all four block options (Potable Water, Shade Access, etc.) to ON.
  6. Confirm each block shows a green background change and a distinct checkmark (✓) icon.
  7. Scroll to Additional Notes and type: Ice chests placed at Zone B scaffold.
  8. Tap "Save Daily Heat Check".
What to watch for: Did the massive calculator collapse cleanly into a compact status bar? Did the checkmark design make the status clear on your phone?
Offline Mode

Scenario 3: Offline Signal Test

Role: Shift Manager Sandboxing: Connection Cut
Story: Your crew shifts into a deep remote sector with zero cell signal coverage. You still must legally cache safety gear parameters.
  1. Swipe your system tray open and switch your mobile phone into Airplane Mode.
  2. Back in the app's Checklist interface, click over to the "PPE & FA" secondary tab form.
  3. Toggle all four safety rows to ON. Tap "Save Safety Gear Check".
  4. Verify the Sync Widget displays 1 Pending Sync or an offline status label.
  5. Turn your phone's Airplane Mode OFF. Click "Sync Now" if it doesn't auto-resolve.
  6. Once resolved to 0, tap "Print Safety Report" at the bottom of the page. Check system print preview.
What to watch for: Did the print preview load successfully, or was it blank? Did all your checked items (Cooling Vests, Hats) show up in the print log?
Briefings

Scenario 4: 5-Minute Shift Briefing

Role: Shift Supervisor Feature: Digital Signatures
Story: The crew is gathered around the service truck bed. You have exactly 5 minutes to deliver a toolkit briefing and register legal proof.
  1. Navigate over to the Briefings tab.
  2. Tap "Save Safety Briefing" immediately on an empty form to stress-test validation behaviors. Note errors.
  3. Under suggested topics, locate "Water, shade, and rest breaks" and click "Use Suggested Topic".
  4. Scroll to attendees, add 3 fake names sequentially (e.g., Tom Diaz, Sarah Miller) using the "+" button.
  5. Sign your name directly inside the canvas signature box, then tap "Save Safety Briefing".
What to watch for: Did the signature track smoothly with zero line lag? Did the saved record cleanly pass down into the "Saved Safety Briefings" log history?
Incident Handling

Scenario 5: Log Active Field Hazard

Role: Crew Operator Sandbox: Permission Swapping
Story: While staging gear, you spot a critical hazard: a mess of high-voltage production lines laid directly across a high-traffic walking lane.
  1. Find the testing dropdown configuration at the top header and swap your profile from "Administrator" to "Worker / Crew Operator".
  2. Tap the Corrective Actions view tab to ensure you are safely blocked out by a permission security gate.
  3. Return to "Today" and click the bright red "Report a Hazard" interface button.
  4. Ensure classification is Hazard. Paste description: High-voltage machinery power cables are tangled directly across the main aisle walkthrough.
  5. Set location to Zone B – Inner Workspace and click "Submit Hazard Safety Report".
What to watch for: Did the submitted card cleanly append onto the shared feed with a yellow Hazard element indicator and a "Review Needed" label?
Resolution

Scenario 6: Resolving Open Issues

Role: Shift Manager Track: Advanced / Wrap Up
Story: You are back in management boots. Review the high-voltage hazard report your field operator just generated, and assign remediation tracking.
  1. Flip your header profile role selector back to "Administrator".
  2. Click onto the Corrective Actions view.
  3. Access the open issues dropdown menu and pick the active cable hazard you generated during Scenario 5.
  4. Assign target ownership to Mark Jackson.
  5. Input remediation details: Coil loose power lines and zip-tie them securely away from walkway traffic.
  6. Select a forward deadline date and click "Assign Corrective Action".
What to watch for: Does selecting "Mark Resolved" clean the open tracking view effortlessly? Was the flow from worker report to manager assignment natural?
